## AUDIT: Playwright Perspective (Story Structure)

### Version A — "The Brew & Co Story"

**Dramatic structure:** ✓ Strong
- **Act 1 (Hook):** Specific result → "here's how" — classic case study opening. Immediate credibility.
- **Act 2 (Journey):** 4-step flow (find → set terms → offers on bills → track). Clear causation. Each step leads naturally to the next.
- **Act 3 (Expansion):** Campaigns, triggers, growth insights — shows the product is deeper than the 4 steps.
- **Act 4 (Close):** "This could be your store" → trust → CTA.

**Pacing:** Good. 9 seconds per screenshot slide is enough to read headline + subtext. The "Here's how" bridge (3 seconds) is tight and effective.

**Emotional arc:** Result → Curiosity → Understanding → Desire → Trust → Action. Correct order.

**Weakness:** Slides 8-10 (campaigns, triggers, growth) come after the main story is told. Risk: viewer mentally checks out after the 4-step flow is complete. They've already decided if they're interested.

**Fix:** Cut campaigns or triggers — pick ONE "go further" slide, not three. The story should be: result → 4 steps → "this could be you" → trust → CTA. Total: 14 slides max, not 17.

### Version B — "The Provocative Question"

**Dramatic structure:** ✓ Strong opening, slightly weaker middle
- **Act 1 (Provocation):** "400 members who've never walked in" — stops the scroll. "What if 20 did?" — creates desire.
- **Act 2 (Answer):** Product intro → find → bills → rules → automate → track. Logical flow.
- **Act 3 (Expand + Close):** Growth, customer, marketplace, trust, invite, CTA.

**Pacing:** The provocation (slides 2-3) is 13 seconds — perfect. Could even be shorter (10s). The middle (slides 5-10) covers 6 product features in 48 seconds — that's a lot. Each gets 8 seconds.

**Emotional arc:** Shock → Desire → "Here's how" → Proof → Trust → Action. Strong.

**Weakness:** Slide 14 ("Know a brand that should be on here? Invite them") breaks the emotional flow. The viewer is in "should I sign up?" mode, and this shifts to "should I refer someone?" Too early. Move invite to post-signup, not pre-CTA.

**Fix:** Cut slide 14 (invite). End with trust → CTA. 15 slides instead of 17.

### Version C — "The Full Platform"

**Dramatic structure:** Complete but risks being a presentation
- **Act 1:** Business truth (2 slides) — demand + gap.
- **Act 2:** 6 product screenshots — the longest section.
- **Act 3:** Customer + marketplace + SA — shows all 3 layers.
- **Act 4:** Trust + scale + CTA.

**Pacing:** 20 slides in 3 minutes = 9 seconds average. That's fast for a comprehensive walkthrough. Some slides (14 — SA dashboard) only get 7 seconds.

**Emotional arc:** Understanding → "That's my problem" → "Here's the solution" → Product depth → Trust → Action. Works but slower to hook.

**Weakness:** The SA dashboard slide (#14) is wrong audience. A retailer doesn't care about platform admin UI. It's impressive to investors, not to buyers.

**Fix:** Cut SA slide for the retailer version. Keep it only in the investor/partner version. Also cut "Free to start. Premium when you're ready" (slide 17) — pricing shouldn't be in a brand video. That's a sales conversation.

## AUDIT: Video Copywriter Perspective (Impact & Clarity)

### Version A — Copy Quality: 8/10

**Best lines:**
- "Got 20 new customers last month. They didn't run a single ad." — Perfect. Specific, surprising, concise.
- "Every FitZone bill now shows Brew & Co's offer." — Makes the mechanic instantly clear.
- "This could be your store next month." — Strong pivot from case study to personal relevance.

**Lines to improve:**
- "Searched by city. Saw fit scores. Sent a proposal." — Too many short sentences. Sounds like a feature list. Better: "Found FitZone in one search. Proposed a partnership. Accepted same day."
- "Pre-approved templates. Delivery tracking. Without ever accessing their customer data." — Three fragments. Sounds technical. Better: "Send WhatsApp offers to their members — without seeing their data."
- "Connect your Shopify, WooCommerce, or website in minutes." — Belongs in a features page, not a story video. Cut or simplify to "Works with your existing tools."

**CTA assessment:** "Turn nearby businesses into your growth network" — Good. Clear, aspirational, action-oriented. 8/10.

### Version B — Copy Quality: 9/10

**Best lines:**
- "The gym 200 metres from your store has 400 members who've never walked in." — The best line in all 3 scripts. Specific, visual, makes you count the businesses around you.
- "What if 20 of them did — this month?" — Perfect follow-up. Creates urgency without being pushy.
- "The gym next door has your next 20 customers. This is how you get them." — Strong CTA echo of the hook.

**Lines to improve:**
- "Search by city. See fit scores. Go live in minutes." — Too terse. Better: "Find the right partners near you and go live in minutes."
- "Pixel, API, or webhook — your choice." — Too technical for a retailer. Cut entirely. "Connect your website or Shopify — it runs automatically" is enough.

**CTA assessment:** "The gym next door has your next 20 customers. This is how you get them." — 9/10. Echoes the hook perfectly. Feels like a complete thought.

### Version C — Copy Quality: 7/10

**Best lines:**
- "Your next customers are already nearby." — True and clear. But heard before.
- "No structured way to turn nearby foot traffic into your customers." — Good problem statement.
- "Every bill, every brand profile, every marketplace visit — brings more customers in." — Shows network effect simply.

**Lines to improve:**
- "Built to handle millions of customers across thousands of brands." — Scale claim without proof. Cut or make specific.
- "Free to start. Premium when you're ready." — Pricing in a brand video breaks the narrative. Save for landing page.
- "Designed for real retail workflows." — Vague. Better: nothing. Let eWards name carry the credibility.

**CTA assessment:** "Turn nearby businesses into your growth network." — Same as Version A. Good but generic. 7/10.

## FINAL RECOMMENDATIONS

### Version A — RECOMMENDED for warm leads / demo follow-ups
- Cut to 14 slides (remove 1 of the 3 "go further" slides)
- Fix the fragment sentences in slides 8-9
- Strongest use: send after a sales call

### Version B — RECOMMENDED for cold outreach / social media
- Cut invite slide (#14)
- Remove technical jargon ("pixel, API, webhook")
- Strongest use: LinkedIn, Instagram, WhatsApp status share
- **This is the most likely to be forwarded by a retailer to another retailer**

### Version C — RECOMMENDED for investor deck / partner pitches
- Cut SA slide and pricing slide for retailer version
- Keep both for investor/partner version
- Strongest use: pitch deck, partnership proposals, eWards internal alignment

### The one change that helps all 3 versions
Replace "Connect your Shopify, WooCommerce, or website" with just "Works with your existing tools." The technical platform names add friction for non-technical retailers.
